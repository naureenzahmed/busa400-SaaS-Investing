op### Investment in SaaS Companies

### Description

Throughout this independent study, I will be exploring how venture capital works and how the standard investment models are adjusted for software as a service companies. The study will also dive deep into the different levels of evaluation metrics, their definitions, factors that play into them and how they are affected by external changes.

The final goal will be to create a website with an overview guide, resources and two market white papers. The white papers will consolidate all the learnings to provide a strong overview on the market’s growth potential. The website will be periodically edited as the content is being created. 

The course will start January 6 till April 11\.

### Learning Outcomes

LO1: Strong ability to explain different types of VC firms, how they operate and how they build their fund thesis.  
LO2: Strong ability to explain portfolio construction at the start of the deployment as well as throughout the lifecycle of the fund.   
LO3: Deep understanding on product, team, market and growth potential metrics from SaaS companies.   
LO4: Ability to apply and evaluate the metrics to any given company and demonstrated through the 2 case studies.    
LO5: Ability to evaluate a market and evaluate the market’s growth potential for future investments. 

### Instructional Methods

The methods will include deep research from industry experts’ writings, academic papers and venture capital firms’ white papers. The research and learning will be consolidated to create a guide on software investing through an online website.
